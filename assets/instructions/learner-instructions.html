In this experiment, you will play the role of a wildlife researcher who is studying fish populations in different lakes. You will visit four lakes that each have 100 fish in them. There are different types of fish in each lake. Your goal is to learn about how many of each type of fish live in the lakes.

The experiment consists of five phases

  1. Reading a message from a previous participant
  2. Catching and observing fish
  3. Predicting the distribution of fish in the lake
  4. Writing a message for the next participant
  5. Predicting the distribution of fish in the lake again

You will earn a bonus that depends on how accurately you predict the distribution of fish.

When you a arrive at a lake, you will read a message from a previous participant. That participant caught fish in the same lake and released them back to learn about the distribution of fish types.

The previous participant was instructed to write a message that helps you learn about the fish in the lake, but they were only able to write a limited number of characters.

After reading a message, you will report your beliefs. You will be asked to predict how many fish of each color there are in the lake, along with how confident you are in that prediction.

You will make a prediction using an interface that looks like this:

Adjusting the confidence slider will move the black bars. You should adjust them so that they cover a range that you are 50% sure contains the true numbers of fish. You can think of the confidence value as the number of fish observed. For example, if you've seen two fish, setting the confidence value to two is a reasonable strategy.

You will earn an individual bonus if the true numbers of fish fall in the range you specify. Your bonus will be larger the smaller your range is (i.e. the more confident you are in your prediction). So it is important to set the confidence slider carefully!

Next, you will go fishing. You will catch a number of fish. After catching a fish, you release it back into the lake. In this phase, you should click the fishing rod to go fishing. You will then see which type of fish you caught.


You will not be able to see which type of fish you caught after you release them, so make sure you remember which fish you caught.

After catching some fish, you will need to leave the lake. Before you leave, you will have the opportunity to write a message for a future participant.

The next participant will also try to learn the distribution of fish in the same lake. However, you will only be able to send small number of characters, which might vary from lake to lake. Different future participants might read different messages, so you can't assume that the same participant will read all of your messages.

Your bonus will depend on the performance of the participants who read your messages, so make sure to write helpful messages!

Finally, you will report your beliefs about the fish in the lake again, using the same interface. You will also earn a bonus based on how well you predict the true distribution of fish in this stage.
